Back in the 90s, the phrase "Plug & Play" gained popularity after it became a Windows 95 term.

The concept tried to address a growing dissatisfaction with compatibility mismatches between devices and software which never seemed to quite work right.

Unfortunately even Plug and Play wasn't quite as reliable as the marketing around it would often suggest, leading to computer users inventing a humorous term: Plug and Pray, which really encapsulated the end-user experience.

---

Since LLMs and generative AI became this huge thing circa 2023 I've been dipping my toes into AI-assisted programming, never quite fully embracing it due to skepticism.

The other night when I was trying to get Claude to help me with some code, a humorous thought struck me. I find that AI assisted coding can be summarized with the term "Prompt and Pray", because that's really what the experience is.

And look it saved my ass a few times, it really did. It found solutions I would not have thought of and was of huge help in certain cases.

However, this is not a consistent experience.
